Vijayawada

Vijayawada is a metropolis city in the amidst of Andhra Pradesh Capital Region, on the banks of Krishna River in Krishna district, of the Indian state of Andhra Pradesh (AP). It is located 5 km (3.1 mi) away from the state capital Amaravati on other side of Krishna river.[5].[6][7] The city is a second largest populous city in AP. ,[8] with a population of 1,034,358 (census 2011).[9]While, the urban agglomeration spread in Guntur district covers, Tadepalle Municipality and its outgrowth of Undavalli; Mangalagiri Municipality and its out growths of Navuluru and Atmakur had a population of 1,491,202. Vijayawada is classified as a Y-grade city as per the Sixth Central Pay Commission. Vijayawada is one of the rapidly growing urban markets of the country.[53] The sectors that contribute to the economy of the city are, construction, education, entertainment, food processing, hospitality, registrations, transport etc.[54] Based on the recommendations of Sixth Central Pay Commission, it is classified as one of the Y-grade cities of India.[14] The GDP of Vijayawada in 2010 was $3 billion and is estimated to touch $17 billion by 2025.[55] The city has trading and exporting markets for agriculture and industrial goods. Hyderabad

Hyderabad is the capital of the southern Indian state of Telangana and de jure capital of Andhra Pradesh.[A] Occupying 650 square kilometres (250 sq mi) along the banks of the Musi River, it has a population of about 6.7 million and a metropolitan population of about 7.75 million, making it the fourth most populous city and sixth most populous urban agglomeration in India. At an average altitude of 542 metres (1,778 ft), much of Hyderabad is situated on hilly terrain around artificial lakes, including Hussain Sagar—predating the city's founding—north of the city centre. Recent estimates of the economy of Hyderabad's metropolitan area have ranged from $40 billion to $74 billion (PPP GDP), and have ranked it either fifth- or sixth- most productive metro area of India.[137] Hyderabad is the largest contributor to the gross domestic product (GDP), tax and other revenues, of Telangana, and the sixth largest deposit centre and fourth largest credit centre nationwide, as ranked by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) in June 2012.[138] Its per capita annual income in 2011 was ?44,300 (US$690).[139] As of 2006, the largest employers in the city were the governments of Andhra Pradesh (113,098 employees) and India (85,155).[140] According to a 2005 survey, 77% of males and 19% of females in the city were employed.[141] The service industry remains dominant in the city, and 90% of the employed workforce is engaged in this sector.
























































Visakhapatnam

Visakhapatnam (also known as Vizag) is the largest city and Financial Capital[5] of the Indian state of Andhra Pradesh.[6] The city is the administrative headquarters of Visakhapatnam district and Eastern Naval Command.[7] Its geographical location is amidst Eastern Ghats mountain range and the coast of Bay of Bengal.[8] It is the most populous city in the state[9][7] and one of the million-plus cities in India,[10] with a population of 2,035,922.[1] The core city and its surrounding settlements together constitute Visakhapatnam Metropolitan Region, one of the most populous urban regions in the country with a population of 5,340,000.[11] The economy of the city is the tenth largest in the country,[12] with a GDP of $43.5 billion. Visakhapatnam is one of the 100 fastest growing cities in the world,[56] which has a GDP of $26 billion.[57] For the FY 2015–16, the per capita income estimates of the city stood at ?140,628 crore (US$22 billion) and it tops among other cities in the state.[58] The fishing harbour is one of the oldest and largest in the city, which was established in 1926, provides livelihood for approximately 50,000 people.[59] The usual seafood exporting capacity of the harbour is 115,000 t (127,000 tons)[60] and during the FY 2015, it topped seafood exports in terms of value among other ports.[61] Visakhapatnam Port and Gangavaram Port are the two ports of the city and the former one topped charts which handled 60,000,000 t (66,000,000 tons) of cargo during the financial year 2016–17.[62] The Hindustan Shipyard undertakes building and repairing of Naval fleet and has future orders of ?2,000 crore (US$310 million).
